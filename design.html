What's our problem?

Given the current COVID-19 pandemic, adolescents find themselves in need of a sense of comfort during a stressful time through comfort entities. However, they often fail to recognize what exactly their comfort entities are, and once they do, they tend to self-undermine its importance, feeling the need to “grow out of it.”


How'd we tackle it?

We followed the 5 stages in the Design Thinking Process: empathy, define, ideate, prototype, and test. Here's how we handled each step:


EMPATHY

Who are the users of this website?


The users are adolescents, more specifically ASHS students, living in the current COVID-19 pandemic. We asked a focus group made up of this demographic a set of questions seen below:

Question 1

Note: If a participant mentions many comfort entities, ask which one is the most current.

Are you a sentimental person? How so?

[if yes] What is something in your life right now that makes you feel sentimental?

[if no] What is something in your life right now that comforts you?

Question 2

Note: Before they answer, explain what a comfort entity is: anything that gives someone a sense of comfort and lessens anxiety from separation (optional: can be physical objects, activities, or concepts such as teddy bears, minecraft worlds, imaginary friends, kins, etc.)

Would you consider this to be your comfort entity?

[if yes, move on to the next question]

[if no] What is something in your life right now that you consider to be a comfort entity?

Question 3

How would you describe your comfort entity? What are its best qualities?

Question 4

What are the specific situations in which you find yourself interacting with this comfort entity? Do you think that this comfort entity has been a big help or a significant part of your quarantine experience?

Question 5

Has this always been your comfort entity?

[if yes] Why?

[if no] What was it before, and what prompted the change?

Question 6

Have you ever found yourself in a situation where a comfort entity was taken from you or you were forced to give it up?

[if yes] What did you feel in that moment? How did you react?

[if no] How would you feel if you were in that situation?

Question 7

Would you ever find yourself giving it up willingly? Why or why not?

After the interviews

As interviewers, we had to make adjustments on our original subject which was Transitional Objects in order to better fit the context of the users and their current setting. We have agreed to focus on the comfort aspect of transitional objects and have also decided to change the name into Comfort Entities in order to widen the reach of what brings a person comfort beyond physical objects.


What are comfort entities, anyways?

A comfort entity is anything that gives someone a sense of comfort and lessens anxiety from separation.


What did we find out?

As we collated the interviews, we read through the answers in order to observe certain patterns or similarities as well as outliers with our goal. Our findings were that most of our interviewees have a comfort entity that they use during the pandemic and that they usually use it because of its availability or the memories attached to it. The interviews also showed what we made prior adjustments to, which was that their comfort entities vary from objects to concepts to activities.
